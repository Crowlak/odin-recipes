Cozido à Portuguesa

Description

This is a traditional and delicious Portuguese stew that features some of the countries famous sausages!

Ingredients

  • 500g/17.64oz Beef prepared for stews (I’d recommend cuts from the chuck)
  • 1 Pork paw
  • Pork ear
  • 300g/10.58oz Pork belly
  • 2 Portuguese Chourizos
  • 1 Blood sausage (without rice)
  • 2 Farinheiras (Portuguese smoked sausage made with flour)
  • 1 Blood sausage (made with rice)
  • 1800g/63.5oz Savoy cabbage
  • 800g/28.22oz Portuguese kale
  • 2 Turnips
  • 4 Potatoes
  • 4 Carrots
  • 300g/10.58oz Rice
  • 1 Beef stock cube
  • 1 Chicken stock cube
  • 1 Vegetable stock cube
  • Salt
  • Cloves
  • 500g/17.64oz Cooked white pea beans

Steps

  1. In a big pan with water, drop the stock cubes, salt, 4 cloves and Portuguese chourizos. Let it boil.
  2. Wash the meats. Add the beef, the pork belly, ear and paw. Let it cook. (Each meat has different cooking times. From first to last to be ready: pork belly - ear and paw – beef).
  3. Wash the vegetables. Once done cooking, remove the meats onto a tray (if you’re following the video, the chef removes the meats separately due to the different cook times of each meat). Add the cabbage and the kale onto the stew and let it boil for 5 minutes. Add the rice blood sausage, the turnips and the carrots cut in four pieces. Let it boil for 5 minutes.
  4. Meanwhile, in another pan, put the rice and add the broth from cooking the meats. Let it cook. Add the potatoes cut in half onto the pan with the turnips and carrots. With a toothpick, make holes on the farinheiras and the remaining blood sausage and add them to the turnip pan. After 4 minutes, remove the blood sausage (without rice).
  5. Take a bit of the broth to a third pan and add the beans. Let it boil for 2 minutes to heat them up properly. After everything is done cooking, cut the meats (in the video, the chef does it while waiting for the vegetables to be done – personal preference) and serve in trays.
